Hamiltonian Path

This page has been set up to replicate the challenge presented by the Clock Puzzles in the video game Final Fantasy XIII-2. These puzzles use a Hamiltonian Path - that is, a "path that visits each vertex exactly once". Nathanial Johnston's Counting and Solving Final Fantasy XIII-2's Clock Puzzles article gives a good insight into the math behind these.

The source can be found on GitHub.

Tutorial

The aim of this game is to fill all the squares by clicking on them. Simple, right? Well not quite! Once you've selected the a square it will no longer be selectable and you will only be able to select the squares adjacent thereafter. The numbers within the squares represent how far away you'll be able to select once that square has been selected. This works in both directions and there are no boundaries to the game board - it loops around. To begin the game, you simply click on any of the squares.
Take for example this set up:

1 1 2 1 3 2 If you click on the 2 in the middle, that square will now be filled and the two squares two to the left and right of it will now be selectable: 1 1 2 1 3 2 If you then clicked on the 1 on the left, the next selectable squares would be the ones to the left and right of that square.
As mentioned before, this loops around, so when this moves to the left, as there are no remaining squares to the left the selection moves on to the 2 on the far right. 1 1 2 1 3 2 If you were to then select the 1 on the left... 1 1 2 1 3 2 ...it would be game over as you can no longer move. :-(
